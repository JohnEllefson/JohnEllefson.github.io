Pecan-Crusted Chicken Strips

Adapted from Fix, Freeze, Feast cookbook (p. 38)

NOTE: Requires time to marinate for at least a day

The marinade is enough for 2-3 cut up chicken breasts (about 14 pieces) in a quart size Ziploc bag, OR you can use 4-6 chicken breasts (22 or more pieces) in a gallon size Ziploc bag, using the same amount of marinade.

  • 2-3 boneless chicken breasts (for a larger family or to make enough for leftovers, you can instead cut up 4-5 chicken breasts)
  • 2/3 cup honey
  • 1/3 cup spicy brown mustard
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 tsp. salt
  • 1 tsp. garlic powder
  • 1 tsp. black pepper

Baking:

  • 2/3 to 1 cup plain Panko (Japanese breadcrumbs)
  • 2/3 to 1 cup pecans, finely chopped (2/3 cup after chopped)

Prepare chicken: Rinse and trim chicken breasts as desired. Cut each breast lengthwise into 3 strips and then each strip into several pieces. You want strips to be about 3 inches long and not too thick.

Prepare marinade: Combine honey, mustard, oil, salt, garlic powder and pepper in a medium bowl. (I spray the measuring cups for the mustard and the honey so it slides out easier after measuring.) Stir together with a whisk. Mix chicken and marinade: Put chicken strips in a quart or gallon freezer Ziploc bag for freezing or in a plastic container if you are going to marinate and use within a couple days. (I place the chicken bag in tall bowl and use a canning funnel and rubber spatula to pour in the sauce. See photos below.) Carefully pour the sauce over the chicken. Squeeze out excess air and zip the bag closed before squishing the sauce and meat strips together. Lay flat in the fridge. I would wait at least 4 hours to overnight (to allow marinade to soak in) before freezing, if desired. I usually let my meat marinade in the fridge 2-3 days before cooking up if I’m not going to freeze it. (Original recipe says to freeze the Panko bread crumbs and finely chopped pecans in a separate bag, but I choose to make it up fresh when I’m ready to cook the meat so they don’t get soggy with condensation in the freezer. I use a Prepworks by Progressive hand nut chopper to chop the pecans.)

To cook: Completely thaw the meat in the fridge (if you froze it). Preheat the oven to 350°. Place Panko crumbs and finely chopped pecans on a plate or in a pasta bowl. Shake excess marinade sauce off each piece of chicken, roll in crumbs, and place on a greased jelly roll pan. (do not use foil on the pan, as the meat may stick to the foil) Bake for 25 minutes, or until chicken pulls apart easily and is no longer pink in the thickest part when cut. Top crust should be starting to brown. Serve with a vegetable and rolls.
