Cooking Tips

Buttermilk substitution:
If you don’t have buttermilk on hand you can use this substitute: Place 1 Tablespoon lemon juice or white vinegar in a liquid measuring cup. Add enough milk to bring the liquid to the one-cup line. Let stand for 5 minutes. Stir together before using. Then use in your recipe that calls for buttermilk. Obviously real buttermilk would be preferred, but it’s always nice to have options when you are in a pinch! (for a thicker buttermilk, add 2 Tablespoons of lemon juice or white vinegar. Using lemon juice is great when making buttermilk for pancakes or muffins, when a lemon flavor would be a nice addition. Use white vinegar when using the buttermilk for something like biscuits or baked chicken. For other ideas for buttermilk substitutes see https://iambaker.net/how-to-make-buttermilk/

Cream of Tartar substitution:
A reviewer of Mrs. Sigg’s Snickerdoodles recipe also suggested a substitute for the cream of tartar, in case you don’t have any on hand: “I was out of cream of tartar and I went to allrecipes.com/advice/ref/subs/ and found out that you can substitute 1 tsp. of cream of tartar with 2 tsp. of lemon juice. I used the lemon juice, and WOW, you'd never know the difference between the recipe with the cream of tartar and the one with the lemon juice! Just a hint for anyone else in the same predicament.”

Herbs (fresh) substitution:
Often, recipes may ask for fresh herbs, and as we all know there are many instances when we don't have them on hand but do have them dried. The flavor's strength is different between fresh and dried herbs, so here is a conversion to help you use just the right amount of an herb: 1 tablespoon chopped fresh herbs equals 1 teaspoon (chopped or whole leaf) dried herbs.
