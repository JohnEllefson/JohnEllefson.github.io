Dinner Rolls

In a large mixing bowl, soften until yeast bubbles up and a sponge forms:

  • 1 Tblsp. yeast (I use instant yeast)
  • 1 cup very warm water (or use very warm milk and omit the powdered milk)
  • 1/4 tsp. sugar

    • Add and mix together with the yeast mixture:

  • 2 cups flour (all-purpose or bread flour—either one works great)
  • 1 1/2 tsp. salt
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 3/8 cup powdered milk (a little less than 1/2 cup)
  • 1 beaten egg
  • 1/4 cup vegetable or canola oil

Then add 1 1/2 cups additional flour

Mix well with a bread dough mixer or by hand. Dough will be a little sticky, but still forms a nice ball. Knead with flour until you can handle the dough but it still is light and a little sticky. Let rise 1 hour. Then you can form the rolls.

Mary uses a lightweight aluminum 9x13 pan, sprayed with olive oil spray. She forms the dough into dinner rolls, lets them rise, and then cooks them at 350° for 12 minutes, then lightly tents foil over the top and bakes for 7 more minutes. She removes them and lets them sit in the pan while sitting on a cooling rack while she rubs the tops with butter. The dough this recipe makes is quite moist and stretchy, and with egg, powdered milk, and extra sugar it has a good flavor to it, fresh out of the oven as well as after a day or so.

Dinner Roll shaping and baking: After removing dough from the bread machine, weigh the dough anddivide into 15 balls, or just divide the dough into fourths and then divide out as equally as you can. Slightly flatten each piece; fold in all four sides, pinch together, then repeat. When nicely shaped, flip over and round into a ball and lightly toss seam side down on the counter to smooth the bottom. Place seam side down in a greased 9 x 13 pan. Repeat with remaining dough, placing balls in 3 rows of 5. Mary uses a greased aluminum pan. Mary makes 15 (3 rows of 5) for rolls large enough to cut open and eat with meat, or to butter and eat with your meal. Cover the shaped rolls and let rise 20-45 minutes (till almost double). Mary usually heats up the oven to 200° then turns it off and opens it and lets it cool a while before placing the pan of shaped rolls covered with a thin towel inside the oven to rise until double. If it is too hot still, leave the oven open a little bit while they rise. Don’t let them rise too much! When the dough is doubled and the rolls have filled in the pan, remove the pan and leave it covered with the towel while you preheat the oven to 350°. Bake at 350° for 12 minutes, and then Mary covers the pan with a loose piece of foil for an additional 7 minutes of baking so the tops don’t get too browned. Rub tops with butter and serve hot! These don’t brown much on the bottoms, but we love them moist just the way they are. These are absolutely fabulous and your family will be in heaven! Best when served hot from the oven! Great for making sandwiches with ham or roast, or eating with butter or jam. Wrap in plastic bags and eat within a few days. (saf-instant yeast is tons cheaper to buy on Amazon—2 lbs. for about $12.)
