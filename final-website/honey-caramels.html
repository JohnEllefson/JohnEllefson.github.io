Honey Caramels

  • 1/2 cup butter
  • 1 can sweetened condensed milk (14 oz.)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup honey (spray measuring cup with cooking spray for easy release of the honey)
  • Chopped walnuts to put on bottom of a 9 to 10 inch pie plate, or two smaller pie plates (we use 1/3 to 1/2 cup chopped nuts in each smaller pie plate) (or you can use pecans if you prefer)

Spray pie plates with cooking spray. Sprinkle chopped walnuts to fill the bottoms of the pie plates. Set aside on a cooling rack or on hot pads. Mix the other four ingredients together in a heavy pot. Stir constantly, using a wooden or heavy silicone spoon, over medium heat. Cook to soft-ball stage (238°). (You can tell when it reaches soft-ball stage by dropping about a teaspoon of the hot mixture into a bowl of very cold water. When the caramel mixture can be formed into a soft ball with your fingers in the cold water, then it has reached the soft-ball stage.)

In 2015, we started making this in a medium sized, heavy Teflon saucepan, using a silicone spoon, or the wooden spoon works fine too. The silicone spoon helps keep the mixture from forming little burned pieces because while constantly stirring it keeps the bottom and sides cleaned. Once the mixture comes to a rapid boil, stir it constantly on medium heat (setting 6 on our home stove) and allow it to boil for 4-5 minutes before checking it for soft-ball stage. Works great! NOTE: The Teflon pan allows for easier cleanup because the caramel mixture literally pours out of the pan instead of sticking. It also helps keep the mixture from getting little burned pieces in it.

Quickly pour into greased pie plate(s) with chopped walnuts on the bottom. (This makes enough to pour into two smaller pie plates with nuts. These are great as gifts, using disposable foil pie pans.) Cool. Cut into squares. Enjoy! I prefer to use two 8-9 inch disposable pie pans and I spray them with olive oil spray instead of buttering them.
