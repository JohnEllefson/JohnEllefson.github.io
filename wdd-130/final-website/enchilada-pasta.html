Enchilada Pasta

Author: Joanna Cismaru, adapted from www.jocooks.com

  • 10-12 oz. uncooked fettuccine noodles
  • 1 Tblsp. olive oil
  • 2 cups cooked chicken chunks, cut against the grain (from rotisserie or cooked breast, cut into pieces)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 cups (or two 10 oz. cans) red enchilada sauce
  • 1/4 cup chicken broth
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 15-oz. can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 3/4 cup frozen corn, steamed and ready to add
  • 1/2 cup finely diced tomatoes (minus the gel/seeds parts)
  • 2 cups Tex Mex cheese (or Colby Jack cheese, shredded)
  • 2 stalks washed and finely sliced green onions

Garnish with:

  • Crispy tortilla strips
  • cilantro for garnish
  • slices of freshly cut lime

Start by boiling the fettuccine until cooked. Drain and set it aside. While the noodles are cooking, rinse and drain the black beans, steam the frozen corn, and wash and cut the onion, green onions, tomatoes, and grate the cheese. In a separate bowl, mix the enchilada sauce, broth, and heavy cream together. In a large skillet heat the oil and add the chopped onion. After the onions are soft, add the minced garlic and cook for another minute. Add the enchilada mixture to the onion mixture in the large skillet. Then add the warm cooked fettuccine noodles. Stir and let it cook until the pasta absorbs some of the sauce and it cooks down a little bit. Then add the warmed chicken chunks, black beans, cooked corn, diced tomatoes, green onions, and the shredded cheese. Stir gently until heated through. The pasta will absorb most of the sauce. Serve with crispy tortilla strips or chips. You can also garnish with cilantro and slices of lime for squeezing over the noodles. If desired, sprinkle additional shredded cheese over the top and place under the broiler for a couple minutes just until the cheese gets golden.
